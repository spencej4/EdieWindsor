Random stuff about this person

"In this memoir, which she began before passing away in 2017 and completed by her co-writer, Edie recounts her childhood in Philadelphia, her realization that she was a lesbian, and her active social life in Greenwich Village's electrifying underground gay scene during the 1050s. Edie was also one of the select group of trailblazing women in computing, working her way up the ladder at IBM and achieving her highest technical ranking while develloping software. In the early 1960s Edie met Theo, an expat from a Dutch Jewish family that fled the Nazis, and a widely respected clinical psychologist. Their partership lasted forty-four years, until Thea died in 2009. Edie found love again, marrying Judith Kasen-Windsor in 2016"

A Wild and Precious Life is a remarkable portrait of an iconic woman, gay life in New York in the second half of hte twentieth century, and the rise of LGBT activism.
